I was born and raised in a small town just south of Portland, Oregon.

I have a brother, David (44), and a sister, Kassidy (23). We were all pretty far apart in age, so we basically grew up as only children. We have a lot of family in Portland though, so we spent a lot of time around our cousins.

I was always in charge of naming the many pets we had growing up. Until I was about ten, almost all of them were named "Skippy".

When I was 18, I moved to Montana to go to college at the University of Montana and graduated with a BA in Cultural Anthropolgy.

After graduation, I decided to stay in the state until I was just shy of 30. During that time, I spent a lot of time outdoors fishing, hiking, camping, and hunting.

I also managed a photography store with a good friend of mine, started (and ended) a small local foods periodical, worked for a local TV station, and became board president of a popular arts nonprofit.

I moved out to Colorado, just north of Boulder, to work at a Google Funded AdTech startup in February, 2013. The business ended up laying of its entire sales force between September and November of 2013.

From there I decided to start my own business, a virtual digital agency, and have run that ever since with a small hiatus in 2014 when I worked for a startup small business lender and moved to Denver.

That break caused my business to pivot from having a dozen-ish contractors and working with midsized businesses doing full-spectrum marketing to being primarily a solo content writing and some low-level front-end web development.

Lacey, we think, is an American Bulldog / Boxer mix. She's the smallest and most dominant of our dogs. She's also by far the most clever.

In the past few weeks, she's learned how to open the doors in our apartment and has regularly taken to letting herself out on the porch when she wakes up in the morning.

Lucky is our oldest dog at twelve, and I've had him since I was 23.

He's slimmed down in his older years, but he weighed in at 100 lbs when he was younger, weighing the same as the other two dogs combined. He's also the most docile.

When Lucky was getting a little older, I decided to get another dog to keep him active. I ended up getting Ringo when he was about a year-and-a-half old after his previous owner had passed away.

In an odd coincidence, we've figured out that his father was a Rhodesian Ridgeback owned by a former client of mine who lives in the same area. His mother was a Yellow Lab.
